# Statutory Rules — Indian Real Estate Law (RERA)

## 1. Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016

### Section 18 — Return of Amount and Compensation
- If a promoter fails to complete or is unable to give possession of an apartment, plot or building:
  - As per the date specified in the agreement for sale; or
  - Due to discontinuance of business as a developer
- The promoter SHALL be liable on demand to the allottee:
  - Return the amount received with interest at prescribed rate (SBI MCLR + 2%) AND compensation
  - OR pay interest for every month of delay until possession is handed over
- The allottee has the RIGHT to choose between refund or continued interest.
- Interest is calculated from the date of each payment made by the allottee.

**Penalty Formula:**
```
Monthly Interest = (Amount Paid × (MCLR + 2%)) / 12
Total Compensation = Monthly Interest × Delay Months
```

### Section 19 — Rights and Duties of Allottees
- Section 19(1): Allottee entitled to obtain information relating to sanctioned plans, layout plans, specifications approved by competent authority.
- Section 19(2): Allottee entitled to know stage-wise time schedule of completion of project.
- Section 19(4): Allottee entitled to claim possession of apartment/plot as per declaration made by promoter.
- Section 19(5): Allottee entitled to claim refund of amount paid with interest and compensation if promoter fails to comply.
- Section 19(6): Allottee required to make payments in time as per agreement.
- Section 19(7): Allottee shall take possession within 2 months of OC (Occupancy Certificate).

### Section 31 — Filing of Complaints
- Any aggrieved person may file a complaint with the Authority or the adjudicating officer.
- Complaints against promoter, allottee, or real estate agent are maintainable.
- No court shall have jurisdiction to entertain any suit or proceeding in respect of any matter which the Authority or adjudicating officer is empowered to determine.
- Complaint must be filed within 3 years of cause of action.
- Filing fee: ₹1,000 to ₹5,000 depending on claim amount.

### Section 11 — Functions and Duties of Promoter
- Section 11(3): Promoter must not accept advance payment exceeding 10% without executing agreement for sale.
- Section 11(4)(a): Promoter must obtain all approvals before advertising or selling.
- Section 11(4)(b): Promoter must maintain separate escrow account — 70% of funds deposited.
- Section 11(5): Promoter must compensate allottee for any loss caused by incorrect information.

### Section 12 — Obligations of Promoter Regarding Veracity of Advertisement
- Promoter liable for any loss suffered by allottee due to false/incorrect information in advertisement.
- Allottee may withdraw from project and claim full refund with interest.

### Section 13 — No Deposit or Advance Without Agreement for Sale
- Promoter cannot accept more than 10% of cost as advance without registered agreement for sale.
- Agreement must specify: possession date, payment schedule, specifications, penalties for delay.

## 2. Penalty Table for Delayed Possession

| Delay Duration | Applicable Rate | Buyer's Option |
|---|---|---|
| 1–3 months | MCLR + 1% per annum | Interest only |
| 3–6 months | MCLR + 2% per annum | Interest or partial refund |
| 6–12 months | MCLR + 2.5% per annum | Interest or full refund |
| > 12 months | MCLR + 2% + 10% compensation | Full refund + compensation |

Current SBI MCLR (1-year): 8.75% (as of April 2026)

## 3. Force Majeure Provisions

Builders may claim exemption ONLY for:
- Natural disasters declared by Central/State Government
- War, civil unrest, or national emergency
- Government-ordered construction stoppage (e.g., NGT orders)
- COVID-19: Extensions granted for 6–9 months (2020–2021 only). NOT applicable post-2022.

Builder must PROVE each month of delay was directly caused by the force majeure event.

## 4. State-Specific Rules — Maharashtra (MahaRERA)

- MahaRERA portal: https://maharera.mahaonline.gov.in
- Complaint filing: Online via portal
- Adjudication timeline: 60 days from filing
- Interest rate applied: SBI MCLR + 2% (simple interest)
- Additional penalty: Up to ₹10,000/day for non-compliance with RERA orders

---

## 5. Key Definitions

- **Allottee**: Person allotted apartment/plot by promoter under agreement for sale.
- **Promoter/Builder**: Entity constructing and selling real estate project.
- **Possession Date**: Date in Sale Agreement for physical handover.
- **Delayed Possession**: Handover after agreed date without written consent of allottee.
- **RERA Authority**: State-level regulatory body established under Section 20.
- **Adjudicating Officer**: Officer appointed under Section 71 to adjudicate compensation claims.
